What Are Golf Cart Batteries?

Golf Cart Batteries are rechargeable energy storage devices that supply electrical power to an electric golf cart’s motor. Most golf carts use a series of batteries connected together to achieve voltages such as 36V or 48V. These batteries work with the motor, controller, and charger to deliver smooth acceleration, hill climbing, and consistent performance across various terrains.

Types of Golf Cart Batteries

Flooded Lead-Acid (FLA) Batteries

Pros: Cost-effective, widely available, easy to replace.

Cons: Requires regular maintenance, water refilling, and terminal cleaning.

Sealed Lead-Acid (AGM) Batteries

Pros: Maintenance-free, spill-resistant, safer indoor use.

Cons: Slightly higher cost than flooded batteries.

Lithium-Ion Batteries

Pros: Lightweight, long lifespan, fast charging, high efficiency.

Cons: Higher upfront cost, requires compatible charger.

How to Choose the Right Golf Cart Battery

  • Correct voltage (36V, 48V, or 72V)
  • Higher amp-hour (Ah) rating for longer rides
  • Proper size and weight compatibility
  • Maintenance preferences
  • Long-term value and performance
